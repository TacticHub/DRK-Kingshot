Troop Formations & Training

Bear Hunt Formation (Standard)

Gen 4+ Players: 1 Infantry / 10 Cavalry / 89 Archers

Archer Lethality is the dominant damage stat in Bear Hunt. Stack Archers to maximize DPS. Infantry provides minimal troop losses due to positioning mechanics.

KvK / PvP Formation

Formation varies by your strongest hero type. Match your troop mix to your rally leader's bonuses. Pure Archer builds work well if your hero is Archer-focused (Prince William, Quinn).

For the full formation cheat sheet, stat priorities, and casualty breakdown — see the Combat Mechanics section below.

Training Priority

  • Always train your highest tier troops — Tier 4+ provide exponentially more power
  • Never let barracks idle — queue troops constantly
  • Balance training with hospital capacity to avoid permanent troop deaths
  • Use Training Speed boosts during alliance training events for bonus rewards
  • Wounded troops heal for free — always fill hospitals before logging off after battles

Combat Mechanics Deep Dive

🎥 Credit This section is based on research by StratGameSloth, a dedicated Kingshot content creator whose videos break down verified game mechanics. Subscribe at youtube.com/@StratGameSloth — all content verified by the DarkKnights community.

How Combat Actually Works (Round-by-Round)

Combat in Kingshot is a hidden simulation — you only see the report, not the logic. Here is the actual sequence every battle follows:

  • 1. Deployment: Troops spawn in order — Infantry → Cavalry → Archers
  • 2. Target Selection: All troops focus the frontmost enemy line. Exception: 20% of Cavalry bypass the frontline every round to snipe Archers directly
  • 3. Damage Resolution: Lethality vs. Health determines how many troops are removed
  • 4. Line Collapse: When a line reaches zero, all attacks immediately retarget the next surviving line
  • 5. Repeat: Attacker and Defender alternate until one side has zero troops

This explains why Infantry and Archers die first — Infantry absorb everything until they fold, while Archers take stray Cavalry charges. Cavalry ironically survive longest because they draw the least aggro until late rounds.

Stat Priority — What Actually Matters

S Tier — Offense
Lethality
Directly removes enemy HP when attacking. The primary kill stat. Prioritize on all damage marches and in research, charms, and hero widgets.
S Tier — Defense
Health (HP)
Total damage a troop absorbs before removal. Infantry have more base HP than Defense — buffing HP gives more raw survivability than equivalent Defense buffs.
A Tier
Defense
Reduces incoming Lethality damage. Important but subject to diminishing returns — HP is safer since Defense can potentially be negated entirely.
B Tier
Attack
Lowers enemy Defense, indirectly boosting your Lethality. Supportive role — invest here only after Lethality and HP are solid.
Currently Useless
Speed
Fixed at 11 for every troop with no known modifier. Do not invest here — zero impact on combat in the current patch.

Stat Investment Order

  • 1st: Archer Lethality — fastest kills, shortens fights before attrition hits. Priority in Research Battle tree, Charms, and hero widgets
  • 2nd: Infantry Health — keeps the frontline alive long enough for Archers to work. Buff HP, not Defense
  • 3rd: Infantry Defense / Archer Attack — secondary layer once the top two are solid
  • 4th: Cavalry — lowest ROI in the current patch; invest only after everything above is maxed
  • Charms vs. Gear: Charms primarily boost Health and Lethality. Most Governor Gear covers Attack and Defense. Choose your upgrade path based on which stat tier you need next

Buff Math — Standard vs. Special (Critical Misconception)

Type A — Standard Buffs (Additive): Sources include Governor Gear, Charms, skins, Alliance research, personal research, and Alliance buildings. These simply add together: two 10% Attack buffs = 20% total.

Type B — Special Buffs (Multiplicative): Sources include hero abilities, exclusive widgets, Kingdom titles (King/Ministers), and consumable 12h/24h army boosts. These multiply on top of your Standard buff total.

Example: With 200% Standard buffs and 40% combined Special buffs, your effective bonus is 320% — not 240%. The stronger your Standard buffs, the bigger the Special buff multiplier becomes. This is why late-game whales scale so aggressively.

Debuffs work as negative Special buffs — disproportionately powerful against high-standard-buff players.

⚠ Consumable Rule — Read This Only the rally leader or garrison defender should use 12h/24h Attack and Defense buff consumables. Rally joiners' consumables do absolutely nothing — their stats don't apply to the rally calculation. Save your items for when you are leading.

Rally Roles — Three Laws

  • The Rally Captain's Build Is Everyone's Build — their heroes, research, skins, consumables, and Kingdom titles apply to the entire rally. Invest in your rally leaders first
  • Joiners Bring Bodies, Not Buffs — joiners contribute troop count and their hero captain skill only. Focus on sending high-tier troops in the correct ratio. Do not burn consumables as a joiner
  • Widgets only come from the rally leader — joiners do not contribute widget bonuses. A hero's widget only activates when that hero is leading the rally or garrison
  • Flat skill bonuses stack; proc-based abilities do not add linearly — two "+25% Lethality" skills add to 50%, but two "40% mitigation" proc skills just give two separate 40% rolls, not 80%

Oasis Island

ℹ Overview Oasis Island unlocks at Town Center Level 19. It runs on two resources: Water Essence (used to upgrade the Fountain of Life and Reservoirs) and Prosperity (a permanent milestone metric increased by placing decorations and buildings). Higher Fountain levels unlock new building types and compound your essence generation over time.

How the Resource Loop Works

  • Fountain of Life: Core structure — upgrade it first and always. Higher levels unlock new building categories and boost passive essence generation.
  • Reservoirs: Actively clear cacti to harvest hidden Water Essence chests. Upgrade to Level 4 ASAP to unlock a second worker and double clearing speed.
  • Prosperity: Increased permanently by placing any decoration or building. Each decoration type has a cap — placing duplicates past the limit grants zero extra prosperity.
  • Essence sources: Passive generation (Fountain + Reservoirs), treasure chests hidden in cacti, island quest rewards, and alliance member assistance bonuses.

Island Tips

  • Each decoration has a Type Limit — placing more than the cap gives zero extra prosperity or buffs. Check before buying duplicates.
  • Clearing cacti from the left side first yields the highest density of treasure chests and essence early — community-verified optimal path.
  • Place Reservoirs near dense cactus clusters; use cosmetic decorations to redirect worker pathing toward priority cacti.
  • Recycling unwanted decorations returns 50% of their essence cost — useful for pivoting to better buildings without a full loss.
  • Prioritize Development buildings (Research Speed, Construction Speed) before Combat buildings for most F2P accounts — the compounding effect outpaces direct stat gains early.
  • Combat buildings (Archer Tower, Command Hall) become the priority once Development buildings are maxed or near-maxed.
  • Coordinate alliance Island building events for collective bonuses, and upgrade during events that award Island-specific rewards.

Events Overview

Events are the fastest way to earn premium resources. Plan your upgrades, training, and research to align with event requirements — doing needed actions during events doubles their value. Event timing can vary by server age and creation date — always cross-reference with the in-game event panel.

Core Recurring Events

  • Cesares Fury: Score points each phase via building upgrades, research, troop training, and gear crafting. Queue your longest upgrades to complete during each fury phase — save speed-ups to double the value.
  • Swordland Showdown: Alliance vs Alliance territorial event. Coordinate rally timing with R4/R5 leaders. Prioritize resource tiles near alliance territory. Discord communication is critical.
  • Growth Missions: Daily and weekly task lists. Complete them for significant free rewards — tasks align with normal gameplay.
  • Gear Events: Craft and upgrade gear during these windows for bonus rewards. Stock materials in advance.
  • Kingdom Events: Server-wide competitions. Focus on your contribution to alliance leaderboard placement.
⚠ F2P Event Rule Never spend gems on event point purchases unless you can reach a reward tier that pays back more gems than you spent. Calculate first, spend second.
✔ Universal Principle Save speed-ups, roulette tokens, hero shards, and upgrade materials for the correct event stage window — spending them outside events loses most of their strategic value.

Bear Hunt (Pitfall Event)

⚠ Never Miss Bear Hunt Forge Hammers — required for Mythic gear upgrades — have no other source. Missing Bear Hunts permanently sets back your gear progression.

How It Works

  • Scheduled by R4/R5 leaders — runs every 2 days with 2 trap slots
  • Before the event: complete Intel Missions to gather Arrowheads and donate to the Bear Trap
  • Event runs for exactly 30 minutes; final rally can be called 5 min before end
  • Rewards are based on personal damage dealt and alliance total contribution

How to Join a Rally

  • Use maximum Archer-heavy formation: 1 / 10 / 89 (Infantry / Cavalry / Archer)
  • Gear only applies to the rally leader — as a joiner, focus on Archer Lethality and Archer Attack from Research and Charms
  • Send secondary marches first with Chenko to boost other rallies' Lethality, then additional joins use Yeonwoo, then Amane
  • Join rallies as fast as possible when they open — every minute of damage counts toward your personal reward
  • Watch Discord for rally call timing — leaders coordinate two trap slots per event

Viking Vengeance

Biweekly alliance defense event — 20 waves of Viking attackers over ~40 minutes. Win by coordinating reinforcements and kill distribution across all active members, not just surviving individually.

Wave Targeting Rules

  • Waves 10 and 20 hit the Alliance HQ — defend these with your strongest heroes and defensive marches
  • Waves 7, 14, and 17 target online members only — be online for these windows
  • If your city burns twice, you are out of targeting but can still score by reinforcing others
  • To successfully defend a wave, you must kill at least 50% of the attackers
  • Shields do NOT work against Viking attacks — do not waste them

The Three Member Non-Negotiables

  • Empty your city before the event and send your troops to reinforce others — a full home city steals kills from reinforcements
  • Do not heal mid-event unless you immediately send healed troops back out — healing returns troops home and disrupts distribution
  • Do not extinguish fires mid-event — this disrupts allied scoring flow

Stamina Tips

  • 24–48 hours before event: do NOT hunt world monsters or spend stamina on small events
  • Save all stamina and stamina potions going into the event
  • Pre-build march presets so you can join rallies instantly when timers open
  • Claim alliance gifts during the event window to supplement stamina

Alliance Championship

Weekly alliance event — six alliances compete across five rounds. Members register into one of three lanes; the alliance that wins two of three lanes each round wins the round.

Member Basics

  • Register into the lane your R4/R5 assigns you — do not switch lanes without leader approval
  • Default troop formation: 50% Infantry / 20% Lancers / 30% Marksman — adjust only if lane captain says otherwise
  • Only the first 20 strongest members per lane count toward the power score — still participate even if you are outside the top 20, as kills still count
  • Follow buff instructions from your leader before registering — the alliance runs a single buff policy for the whole event
  • Be ready to fight at round start time — early KOs set the tempo for the round

Hall of Governors

ℹ Note Hall of Governors is primarily a first- and second-generation kingdom event. Later kingdoms may see it replaced by Strongest Governor (a cross-kingdom leaderboard format).

What Scores Big Points

Hero Roulette spins are the largest single point spike — each spin awards ~90,000 points. Save all roulette tokens for the Hero Development stage.

Troop training scales heavily by tier — high-tier troops earn dramatically more points per unit. Train during the Train Troops stage only.

Member Stage Rules

  • Construction & Research stages: Queue long upgrades to complete during the stage; use alliance help to shorten timers
  • Hero Development stage: Hold all roulette tokens and hero shard ascensions until this stage opens — highest point value
  • Train Troops stage: Train only during this stage; higher tier troops earn far more points per unit
  • Gear & Charm stages: Stockpile upgrade materials in advance and spend them only when the correct stage is active
  • Check with your R4/R5 before spending large resources — the alliance may be running a coordinated push that requires certain members to hold off
✔ F2P Member Rule Focus on hitting milestones efficiently. Do not compete with designated pushers on troop day or roulette day — your alliance will communicate who is pushing vs. milestone-only each cycle.

All Out (Kill Event)

Two-day PvP event running every few weeks. Points are earned by defeating or severely wounding enemy troops via city attacks, tile hits, or defending attacks. Points scale by troop tier — higher-tier enemies are worth more per kill.

Safe Scoring Strategy (Recommended for F2P)

✔ Low-Risk Method Attack resource gathering tiles instead of cities. Tile hits generate points without risking direct troop deaths. City attacks can cause permanent troop deaths — only do them if you are prepared to absorb losses.
  • Keep a shield active whenever you are offline or not actively fighting — non-negotiable during peak hours
  • Garrison troops in alliance structures when idle rather than leaving them in your city
  • Scout enemy cities before attacking — check hospital capacity to estimate risk
  • Respect your server's community-enforced rules (NAPs, territory restrictions, rally bans) — violating them invites large-scale retaliation

Recovery After Losses

Batch healing: Heal in smaller increments so alliance help can reduce timers efficiently, rather than burning speed-ups and gems on large single heal blocks. This is often the difference between stabilizing an account and quitting after a bad All Out.

Moonlit Promise

ℹ Note Moonlit Promise is a month-long Ramadan special event. Do not confuse it with "Moonlit Celebration," which is a separate festival-style event with different mechanics and timing.

How It Works

  • Radiant Wishes: Use Crescent Seekers to light lanterns and earn Crescent Emblems with multipliers (1x 60% / 2x 20% / 3x 10% / 5x 6% / 10x 4%)
  • Ally Blessings: After lighting 30 lanterns, receive 1 Mythic Hero Shard and share 2 Crescent Emblems with members — up to 20 blessing receives per day (resets daily)
  • Crescent Bazaar: Spend Crescent Emblems on Mythic/Lucky gear, Governor Gear Chests, Forge Hammers, Charm Designs, speed-ups, and emotes
  • End-of-event: Unused Crescent Emblems and Seekers convert to food resources — spend down before the event ends

Shop Priority (F2P)

The shop does not reset daily, so plan your full-event purchase order rather than optimizing day-by-day. Community consensus: Forge Hammers are the highest-value F2P buy. Gear and charm-related items come next, especially if Hall of Governors is upcoming — buy now, but delay the actual upgrade until the correct HoG stage.

Alliance Coordination Protocol

  • Set a daily "Blessings open" posting rule — members post in Discord after hitting the 30-lantern threshold so others can claim ally blessings before the daily cap
  • Blessing limits reset daily — coordinate timing so members claim from as many active blessers as possible each day
  • Set a daily target for mission completion — consistent daily play is the main limiter, not spending
  • Post an end-of-event reminder to spend remaining currency before it converts to bread

Kingdom vs Kingdom (KvK)

ℹ Overview KvK is the largest inter-kingdom battle event. Your server competes against others for territory and prestige. Follow your R4/R5 instructions closely — individual actions during KvK affect the whole alliance.

Member Survival Rules

  • Shield up whenever you are offline or not actively fighting — unshielded cities are easy targets during KvK
  • Keep troops garrisoned or in hospital when you log off during active KvK phases
  • Gather on your own server tiles before KvK starts — cross-server gathering resets when you cross over
  • Stack near your alliance zone — solo cities far from allies get picked off first
  • Scout before attacking — check hospital capacity, troop count, and shield status on any target
  • Follow rally call timing from your R4/R5 — solo attacks rarely crack strong cities and waste troops

Eternity's Reach

Stage-based solo event that awards Charm Items — the primary resource for Charm upgrades, which provide massive late-game combat stat multipliers. Prioritize this event for long-term power growth.

Stage Strategy

Early Stages 1–5

Use your strongest combat heroes. Focus on clearing stages before the event timer expires. Attempt stages daily.

Mid Stages 6–10

Hero level and star upgrades become critical. Pair Lethality heroes with Archer-boosting secondary heroes.

Hard Stages 11–15

Requires maxed heroes and high Charm bonuses. Top-tier F2P players can reach these with a focused strategy.

Elite Stages 16+

Predominantly spender territory. F2P: focus on consistent mid-stage completion for steady Charm accumulation.

Hero Recommendations

  • Primary: Your highest-star Archer rally hero (Prince William, Amadeus)
  • Support: Chenko for Lethality aura — place in secondary march
  • Utility: Quinn for Archer Attack % bonus on difficult stages
  • Upgrade Charms in order: Attack > Lethality > HP > Defense
✔ F2P Tip Even reaching Stage 8–10 consistently earns enough Charms to stay competitive. Don't skip this event — Charm materials have no other primary source.

F2P Strategy Guide

✔ Core F2P Philosophy F2P success in Kingshot is about consistency and efficiency, not speed. Log in daily, complete every event task you can, and never waste resources on low-value targets.

Gem Spending Priority (F2P)

  • 1st: VIP Level — the single best gem investment. Every VIP level adds permanent daily bonuses
  • 2nd: Speed-ups during Cesares Fury / Building Events — spend to earn back more than you spent
  • 3rd: Hero Shards when a strong hero is on rotation (Prince William, Chenko, Quinn)
  • Never: Resource packs, random chests, or PvP shields outside of KvK season

Daily F2P Checklist

  • Collect VIP points and daily login rewards
  • Queue building upgrade + research
  • Send troops gathering on resource tiles
  • Complete Growth Missions and Daily Tasks
  • Run hero expeditions and collect rewards
  • Check event tasks — align actions with event point requirements
  • Heal wounded troops (always free)
  • Donate to alliance technology

Kingdom Transfer Event

ℹ Overview Every 40–60 days a transfer window opens, allowing players to move their entire account to a different kingdom. Troops, resources, heroes, and items all transfer — your map position resets. Alliance membership does not auto-transfer.

Why Transfer?

  • Alliance consolidation: Reunite with allies or move to a kingdom where DarkKnights already has strong footing
  • Escape a dominated kingdom: If your server has no realistic path to competition against entrenched alliances
  • KvK bracket optimization: Younger kingdoms are matched against similarly-aged kingdoms — transferring can reset your bracket to a fairer matchup
  • Use the Transfer Planner to coordinate large group moves: v2.kingshot.net/transfer-planner

Transfer Rules & Restrictions

  • Target kingdom must be within an eligible age bracket relative to your own kingdom
  • Your TC level may impose minimum or maximum kingdom age requirements for the destination
  • Clear all active building, research, and training timers before transferring if possible
  • Coordinate with your destination alliance leadership to receive an invite immediately on arrival
  • Check kingdom ages at v2.kingshot.net/kingdom-age-tracker — 426+ kingdoms in the database

DarkKnights Transfer Protocol (K#1640)

⚠ R4/R5 Approval Required Any transfer must be communicated to R4/R5 before initiating. Uncoordinated transfers weaken alliance power scores and can hurt Hall of Governors and Championship seeding. R5 maintains the official approved-destination list for each transfer window.
  • Assign a scout to the destination kingdom 48 hours early to assess the political landscape (dominant alliances, existing NAPs, activity level)
  • Coordinate timing so the bulk of transferring members arrive within the same day
  • Use the Transfer Planner to organize member groups and arrival order
  • Post arrival confirmation in Discord so leadership can send alliance invites immediately

✔ Live Codes at the Top Active codes are tracked in the Gift Codes banner just below the page header. Click ✎ Edit Codes to add or remove codes — changes are saved in your browser. Click any code pill to copy it instantly.

How to Redeem

Option 1 — Web: v2.kingshot.net/gift-codes/redeem — enter your Player ID and paste the code. Rewards arrive in in-game mail.

Option 2 — Auto Redeem: v2.kingshot.net/auto-redeem — applies all currently known active codes in bulk.

Option 3 — In-game: Settings → Gift Code → enter code directly.

Where to Find New Codes

  • Official Kingshot Discord (announcements channel)
  • Kingshot social media — Facebook, Twitter/X, YouTube livestreams
  • v2.kingshot.net/gift-codes — community list, updated within hours of any new release
  • DarkKnights Discord #resources channel — R4/R5 post new codes as soon as they drop; update the banner above so all members see them
