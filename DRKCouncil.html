Bear Hunt — Alliance Management

ℹ Context Members handle their own rally joining and formation. This section covers the R4/R5 scheduling and trap management responsibilities.

Scheduling & Trap Triggers

  • Poll members for best time slots before setting the trap — attendance is the biggest damage multiplier
  • Trigger both traps at different times to maximize participation across time zones
  • Event runs for exactly 30 minutes; the final rally can be called up to 5 minutes before the end
  • Remind members to complete Intel Missions and donate Arrowheads to the trap before it fires

Joiner Cap Formula

To prevent instant-fill and distribute damage fairly across members, set a per-member troop limit when opening the rally:

Cap = (Rally Capacity − Leader Squad Capacity) ÷ Expected Number of Joiners

Set this as the joiner troop limit when launching the rally. Adjust down if more members than expected are online.

Rally Leader Requirements

  • Full gear equipped on the rally leader — gear only applies when leading, not joining
  • Use max Archer-heavy formation: 1 / 10 / 89 (Infantry / Cavalry / Archer)
  • Prioritize Archer Lethality and Archer Attack from Gear, Island, Research, and Charms
  • Deploy secondary marches first with Chenko to boost other rallies' Lethality, then additional joins use Yeonwoo, then Amane

Viking Vengeance — Leader Execution Plan

20 waves over ~40 minutes. Waves 10 and 20 hit the Alliance HQ. Waves 7, 14, and 17 target online members only. The leader's job is to coordinate reinforcement assignments, rally chains, and HQ defense timing before the event starts.

Pre-Event Setup

  • Schedule start time to maximize online attendance for waves 7, 14, and 17
  • Create reinforcement pairs or triads in Discord before the event — every city should have designated incoming support
  • Post a Discord listing of rally leaders and their reinforcement assignments before the event fires
  • Ensure all members have read the three non-negotiables: empty city, no mid-event healing, no extinguishing fires

HQ Defense Timing

  • Reserve your strongest heroes for HQ defense at waves 10 and 20
  • Recall key marches around waves 9 and 19 so they arrive back in time for the HQ hits
  • Assign your highest-power members to HQ reinforcement duty for these two waves

Rally Chain Execution

  • Run continuous rally chains with no gaps: Leader 1 engages, Leader 2 prepares, Leader 3 prepares next
  • Communicate wave numbers in Discord so members know when to reinforce vs. when to hold
  • Members who have burned twice can no longer be targeted but can still reinforce — redirect them to support active defenders

Stamina Management

  • Announce a stamina lockdown 24–48 hours before the event — no world monster hunting, no stamina spending
  • Target: 3,000–6,000 stamina per active member going into the event
  • Remind members to claim alliance gifts during the event window to supplement stamina

Alliance Championship — Lane Command

Win condition: take 2 of 3 lanes each round. The strategic imperative is building two dominant lanes and deliberately sacrificing one. Do not spread strength evenly across all three.

Lane Assignment Strategy

Strong Lanes 1 & 2
Stack Your Best
Alternate your top-power members across Lanes 1 and 2. These are your win lanes.
Sacrifice Lane 3
Accept the Loss
Fill with remaining members. Align this lane against the opponent's strongest lane intentionally.
  • Treat round 1 as scouting — use visible opponent lane history from rounds 1–2 to counter-stack in rounds 3–5
  • Assign a single lane planner for the full event — multiple officers reshuffling independently destroys coordination
  • Only the first 20 strongest members per lane count for the power score — plan assignments around this cap
  • A single whale cannot carry alone — individual KO limits apply per round

Registration Buff Policy

⚠ Alliance Decision Required Sources are split. Registering fully buffed may lock in higher fight strength. But some reports indicate it inflates matchmaking power into harder brackets. Pick ONE policy and enforce it for a full cycle. Inconsistency within the alliance is worse than a slightly suboptimal choice.
  • Winning your tier comfortably → register with buffs active
  • Consistently matched against far stronger alliances → trial registering without temporary buffs for one full cycle and compare results

Hall of Governors — Leader Operations

ℹ Note Primarily a first- and second-generation event. Later kingdoms may see Strongest Governor instead. Stage structures differ across iterations — verify stage order each cycle before communicating to members.

Choose Your Alliance Mode Each Cycle

Mode A — Default
Milestone-First
Everyone clears best milestones without draining long-term stockpiles. Announce this clearly so members don't waste resources competing with each other.
Mode B — Coordinated Push
Leaderboard Push
Designate 1–3 pushers to spike on troop day and roulette day. All other members do milestones only — communicate explicitly who is pushing so others don't interfere.

Stage-by-Stage Leader Checklist

  • Construction & Research stages: Remind members to queue long upgrades that will complete during the stage; encourage maximum alliance help usage
  • Hero Development stage: Announce the stage opening — this is when roulette tokens and hero shard ascensions are worth the most points
  • Train Troops stage: Pre-coordinate training capacity with members; high-tier troops earn dramatically more points per unit
  • Beast Slay / Gather stages: Coordinate rally schedules and communicate stamina expectations
  • Gear & Charm stages: Remind members to hold upgrade materials until the correct stage is active
✔ Leaderboard Push Calculation Pushing for the season hero is only justified early-server when it meaningfully improves rally strength. Mid-server, milestone-first is usually the correct call — top-rank rewards are shard-gated and the shard types don't always apply where members need them.

All Out — Leader Pre-Event Checklist

All Out is the event most likely to cause member frustration, permanent troop loss, and alliance conflict if not properly managed before it starts. Leader communication beforehand prevents the majority of problems.

Pre-Event Announcements Required

  • Post your alliance's current All Out rules in Discord before the event opens — include NAPs, rally ban lists, and territory restrictions
  • Explicitly warn newer members: city attacks can permanently kill troops in ways PvE players don't expect
  • Remind members to shield up immediately when going offline during the event — non-negotiable
  • Remind members to garrison troops in alliance structures when idle rather than leaving them in their city
  • Remind members to scout before attacking — check hospital capacity to estimate whether a city is safe to hit

Safe Scoring Guidance to Share with Members

  • Recommend tile hitting over city attacks for F2P members — tile hits generate points without permanent troop loss risk
  • Post your server's political landscape (active NAPs, hot zones) so members don't accidentally create diplomatic incidents

Post-Event Recovery

Advise members who took heavy losses to batch heal in small increments so alliance help can reduce timers efficiently, rather than burning speed-ups and gems on large single heal blocks. This is often the difference between a member stabilizing vs. quitting after a bad All Out.

KvK — Command Operations

ℹ Overview KvK is won or lost at the alliance leadership level. Member guidance covers personal survival rules. This section covers rally coordination, target selection, and territory control decisions.

Rally Coordination

  • Coordinate rally timing centrally — solo attacks rarely crack strong cities and waste troop capacity
  • Focus fire on enemy cities with low hospital capacity — this forces permanent troop loss on the enemy
  • Stack alliance members in the same zone for mutual reinforcement and rally depth
  • Assign dedicated rally leaders before each push phase — identify who leads and who joins

Territory Control

  • Prioritize holding resource-rich tiles near your alliance cluster — feeds the war effort and denies enemy income
  • Establish a hive formation around your HQ — clustered cities reinforce each other faster and deter solo attacks
  • Identify enemy alliance HQ location early — this is the highest-value target during active KvK phases
  • Assign border watchers to alert the alliance when enemy columns are moving toward your zone

Phase-by-Phase Priorities

Preparation Phase
Stack & Gather
Members gather on home tiles, relocate into hive, and fill hospitals. No early aggression.
Active Phase
Rally & Hold
Coordinate rally chains on priority targets. Maintain zone control. Reinforce under-pressure members.
Wind-Down
Recover & Shield
All members shield up. Batch heal. Assess losses. Debrief in Discord for next cycle.
